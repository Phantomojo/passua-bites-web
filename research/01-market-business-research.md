## 1. Kenyan Food Delivery Market Analysis

### Market Size & Growth
- Kenya's food delivery market is growing rapidly, driven by smartphone penetration (85%+ Android) and mobile payment adoption
- Major urban centers: Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, Nakuru, with Ruiru as a growing satellite town
- Food delivery apps gained significant traction post-2020, with sustained growth through 2026

### Major Players

| Platform | Market Position | Commission Rate | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Bolt Food** | Market leader in Kenya | 25-35% | Strong brand, wide coverage, aggressive marketing |
| **Uber Eats Kenya** | Strong competitor | 25-30% | Global brand, integrated with Uber rides |
| **Glovo Kenya** | Active in Nairobi | 25-35% | Multi-category delivery |
| **Jumia Food** | Declining presence | 20-30% | Previously dominant, now reduced operations |

### Consumer Behavior
- **44.8% of Kenyan e-commerce orders happen via apps/WhatsApp** (Biznake 2026)
- WhatsApp ordering is deeply entrenched - Naivas, Twiga Foods, countless restaurants use it
- Phone call ordering still common for local businesses
- Website ordering is growing but still secondary to app/WhatsApp for many users

### Key Insight for Passua Bites
Your website should **complement** Bolt Food, not compete directly. Use it for:
- Direct orders (save 25-35% commission)
- Customer relationship building
- Brand ownership
- Higher margins on repeat customers

## 2. Payment Landscape

### M-Pesa Dominance
- M-Pesa handles **90%+ of mobile money transactions** in Kenya
- Safaricom's M-Pesa is the default payment method for Kenyan e-commerce
- Alternative: Airtel Money (smaller market share), card payments (growing but still niche)

### M-Pesa Transaction Fees (2025)
Customer charges for sending money:
- KES 1-100: Free
- KES 101-500: KES 7
- KES 501-1,000: KES 13
- KES 1,001-1,500: KES 23
- KES 1,501-2,500: KES 33

**Recommendation:** Use **Business Bouquet** tariff plan so business absorbs fees - customers aren't charged extra.

### Payment Methods to Support
1. **M-Pesa STK Push** (primary - seamless checkout)
2. **Cash on Delivery/Pickup** (fallback - important for trust)
3. **M-Pesa Manual Payment** (C2B - show Paybill/Till number as backup)

## 3. Regulatory & Legal Requirements

### Kenya Data Protection Act (2019)
**Key Requirements:**
- Register as a data controller with Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)
- Obtain explicit consent before collecting personal data
- Encrypt personal data at rest (phone numbers, names, addresses)
- Implement data retention policies
- Provide customers right to access/delete their data
- Report data breaches within 72 hours

**For Passua Bites:**
- Privacy Policy required (explain what data you collect and why)
- Terms of Service required
- Cookie consent banner (if using analytics cookies)
- Secure storage of customer phone numbers (personal data)

### Business Licensing
- County business permit (Kiambu County for Ruiru)
- Food handling certificate (from public health department)
- Single business permit

### Tax Obligations
- **VAT:** 16% on taxable supplies (registration mandatory if annual turnover exceeds KES 5 million)
- **Income Tax:** Based on business structure (sole proprietor vs limited company)
- **Digital Service Tax:** 1.5% on digital services (may apply to online platforms)
- **KRA iTax:** All filings done through iTax portal
- Keep records of all M-Pesa transactions for reconciliation

### Food Safety
- Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) compliance
- Food hygiene certificate for all handlers
- Regular health inspections

## 5. Customer Psychology

### Trust Factors for Kenyan Online Food Ordering
1. **M-Pesa payment badge** - most important trust signal
2. **Physical location displayed** - "Ruiru, along Thika Road" with map
3. **Phone number prominently shown** - expected to be reachable
4. **Real photos of actual stall** - not stock photos
5. **Social media presence** - "5K+ followers on TikTok"
6. **Google Reviews** - star rating visible

### Price Sensitivity
- Ksh 40-200 range is street food territory
- Customers are highly price-aware
- Delivery fees matter significantly
- Direct orders should be cheaper than Bolt Food (no commission)

### Delivery Expectations
- **Ruiru vs Nairobi CBD:** Ruiru customers expect faster, cheaper delivery
- Peak hours: Lunch (12-2pm), Dinner (6-8pm), Late night (9-11pm)
- 30-45 minutes is acceptable for delivery
- Free delivery above certain amount incentivizes larger orders

### Mobile-First Behavior
- Android dominance (94%+)
- Data costs matter (users on limited bundles)
- Loading speed critical (3G reality for many)
- WhatsApp "free" bundles make it popular

### Social Proof Importance
- Reviews with star ratings essential
- TikTok/Instagram influence purchasing
- "John in Ruiru just ordered 3 smokies" social notifications
- Word-of-mouth amplified digitally

## 7. Delivery Logistics

### Delivery Radius
- Recommended: **5km radius** from Rainbow Resort, Ruiru
- Key areas: Ruiru town, Juja, Kahawa, Githurai, Roysambu
- Beyond 5km: delivery fees become prohibitive for street food prices

### Delivery Fee Structure Options
1. **Flat rate:** Ksh 50-100 within radius (simple, predictable)
2. **Distance-based:** Ksh 50 for <2km, Ksh 100 for 2-5km (fairer)
3. **Free above amount:** Free delivery for orders over Ksh 500 (incentivizes larger orders)
4. **Hybrid:** Ksh 50 flat, free above Ksh 300 (recommended)

### Delivery Personnel Options
- **In-house riders:** Hire 1-2 riders on motorcycle/bicycle (more control, higher cost)
- **Third-party:** Use Bolt delivery, Sendy, or local riders (flexible, less control)
- **Customer pickup:** Always available as zero-delivery-cost option

### Peak Hours Management
- **Lunch rush:** 12:00-14:00 (office workers, students)
- **Dinner:** 18:00-20:00 (families, workers returning home)
- **Late night:** 21:00-23:00 (young adults, entertainment crowd)
- Pre-prep during slow periods
- Communicate delays proactively

## 9. Customer Retention

### Loyalty Programs That Work in Kenya
- **Stamp card:** Buy 9, get 10th free (digital version)
- **Points system:** Ksh 100 spent = 1 point, 10 points = Ksh 50 off
- **VIP tier:** Orders above Ksh 2000/month get 10% discount
- **Referral bonus:** Refer a friend, both get Ksh 50 off

### SMS Marketing
- Still very effective in Kenya
- Use sparingly (1-2 messages per week max)
- Promotions, new items, special offers
- Opt-in required (compliance)

### WhatsApp Broadcast Lists
- Customers opt-in
- Weekly menu updates
- Daily specials
- Order reminders ("Haven't ordered this week!")

### Order Frequency Optimization
- Target: 2-3 orders per week per active customer
- "Your usual" reorder feature
- "Miss you" offers after 7 days of inactivity
- Subscription model (weekly smokie delivery?)

## 10. Financial Planning

### M-Pesa Transaction Fees
- Business Bouquet: Business absorbs fees
- Factor into pricing: Ksh 7-33 per transaction depending on amount
- For Ksh 70 smokie: Ksh 7 fee = 10% of item price
- Consider minimum order amount to make fees worthwhile

### Revenue Projections (Conservative Estimate)
Assuming 20 orders/day average at Ksh 200 average order value:
- Daily revenue: Ksh 4,000
- Monthly revenue: Ksh 120,000
- Annual revenue: Ksh 1,440,000

**Moderate Estimate:** 40 orders/day
- Monthly revenue: Ksh 240,000
- Annual revenue: Ksh 2,880,000

**Aggressive Estimate:** 80 orders/day
- Monthly revenue: Ksh 480,000
- Annual revenue: Ksh 5,760,000 (VAT registration threshold approaching)

### Cash Flow Management
- M-Pesa settlements: Usually instant to your M-Pesa account
- Separate business and personal M-Pesa accounts
- Track all expenses (ingredients, delivery, platform costs)
- Monthly reconciliation with M-Pesa statements

### Tax Obligations
- **Income Tax:** Based on profit (revenue minus expenses)
- **VAT:** Mandatory if annual turnover exceeds Ksh 5M
- **Record Keeping:** Keep all receipts, M-Pesa statements, invoices
- **Accountant:** Consider hiring one for proper tax planning

## 11. Risk Management

### Food Safety & Liability
- Food poisoning incidents can destroy reputation
- Proper food handling and storage
- Temperature control during delivery
- Customer complaint handling procedure
- Consider liability insurance

### Payment Fraud Prevention
- Verify M-Pesa payments before fulfilling orders
- Check transaction IDs match
- Watch for duplicate payment claims
- Keep records of all transactions

### Delivery Risks
- Rider accidents (insurance needed)
- Order theft (rider runs with food/money)
- Customer disputes (wrong order, late delivery)
- Weather disruptions (rainy season)

### Platform Risks
- Website downtime (have WhatsApp fallback)
- Data breaches (secure customer data)
- M-Pesa API downtime (have cash fallback)
- Domain/hosting issues (reliable provider)

### Supply Chain Risks
- Ingredient price fluctuations
- Supplier reliability
- Seasonal availability
- Have backup suppliers

### Insurance Considerations
- Business insurance
- Rider insurance (motorcycle accidents)
- Food liability insurance
- Equipment insurance

## Key Takeaways

1. **Direct orders save 25-35%** vs Bolt Food commissions - pass some savings to customers
2. **WhatsApp is critical** - 44.8% of Kenyan e-commerce involves WhatsApp
3. **M-Pesa is non-negotiable** - must support STK Push as primary payment
4. **Mobile-first design** - 94% Android, design for 360px width first
5. **Data costs matter** - keep site lightweight, under 500KB initial load
6. **Trust signals essential** - M-Pesa badge, location, phone, real photos
7. **Delivery radius 5km** - beyond that, fees eat into margins
8. **Peak hours matter** - prep for lunch (12-2pm) and dinner (6-8pm) rushes
9. **Social proof drives sales** - TikTok, Instagram, Google Reviews
10. **Start small, scale gradually** - prove model before expanding
